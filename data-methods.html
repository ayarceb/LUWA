LUWA integrates satellite observations, atmospheric transport modelling and lightweight emission-estimation methods. The workflow combines TROPOMI-based products, meteorological data, data assimilation and LOTOS-EUROS simulations to support source-level environmental analysis.

Data Assimilation

Integration of observations and model outputs to improve the representation of atmospheric composition, transport and source influence.

Satellite Observations

Use of satellite products, including TROPOMI-based observations, to detect spatial patterns of NO2, SO2, NH3 and related atmospheric signals.

Emission Estimation

Application of lightweight methods and inverse-modelling concepts to estimate emissions from point sources, industrial areas and agricultural systems.

LOTOS-EUROS on AWS

Atmospheric chemistry-transport simulations using LOTOS-EUROS workflows deployed in cloud infrastructure for controlled environmental scenarios.

Plume Diagnostics

Analysis of plume direction, dispersion, source attribution and meteorological drivers using satellite data and wind fields.

Computational Planning

Estimation of simulation domain size, CPU needs and operational feasibility for cloud-based atmospheric modelling experiments.

TNO LOTOS-EUROS IMI System

LOTOS-EUROS simulation inputs for Europe and Mexico

This table shows the data families required to launch a standard LOTOS-EUROS run. It is written for decision-making: what enters the model, what LUWA checks, and what Humath receives through a controlled SaaS/API workflow.

What it means for Humath

Controlled access

Humath consumes outputs without receiving LUWA source code, credentials, Docker internals or model configuration secrets.

Transparent inputs

The client can see meteorology, boundaries, emissions, land cover and surface data used to build the run.

Cost drivers

Runtime depends mainly on domain size, resolution, simulation days, output cadence and active modules.

Delivery cadence

Outputs can be configured as hourly or 3-hourly, depending on the analysis need and storage budget.

Weather

ECMWF meteorology drives winds, mixing, clouds, rain, chemistry and deposition.

Emissions

Anthropogenic, biogenic, dust, sea-salt and fire emissions define what enters the atmosphere.

Surface

Land use, topography, soil and land-sea mask control roughness, deposition and natural sources.

Boundaries

CAMS/MACC, climatologies, EMEP, TM5 or parent LOTOS-EUROS runs provide background air entering the domain.

Europe / MACC domain

Simulation window: 2024-08-12 to 2024-08-22.

  • Preset: MACC / default LOTOS-EUROS European domain.
  • Bounds: West 15.0°W, East 35.0°E, South 35.0°N, North 70.0°N.
  • Grid: 0.50° longitude × 0.25° latitude; 100 × 140 cells.
  • Output: hourly or 3-hourly NetCDF products.

LUWA Mexico domain

Simulation window: 2024-07-01 to 2024-07-27.

  • Provided pilot box: North 24.0°N, South 23.0°N, West 103.0°W, East 102.0°W.
  • Grid: resolution selected according to input availability and cost target.
  • Setup: non-European domain using global meteorology, global boundaries and global land-cover support.
  • Output: hourly or 3-hourly NetCDF products.

How to read this table: Europe uses the standard MACC-domain logic. Mexico uses the same LOTOS-EUROS workflow, with domain-specific inputs for land cover, emissions and boundaries. The table excludes a satellite-check row because this section is focused on the model launch package rather than independent validation.

Anthropogenic emissions Represents human activity: power generation, industry, traffic, shipping, aviation, agriculture, solvents and waste.
GNFR / MACC sector names
  • A: public power stations.
  • B: industry.
  • C: commercial and institutional combustion.
  • D: extraction and distribution of fossil fuels.
  • E: solvents.
  • F: road transport; F1 gasoline exhaust, F2 diesel exhaust, F3 other fuels, F4 evaporation plus road, brake and tyre wear.
  • G/H/I: other mobile sources, including shipping, aviation and other transport.
  • J: waste treatment and disposal.
  • K/L: agriculture, livestock and other agricultural sources.

  • CORINE/Smiatek classes: urban areas, agriculture, grassland, deciduous forest, coniferous forest, mixed forest, water, marsh/wetland, sand/bare rocks, tundra, permanent ice, tropical forest and woodland shrub.
  • DEPAC classes: grass, arable land, permanent crops, coniferous forest, deciduous forest, water, urban, other, desert and short grassy tundra.

Biogenic emissions Adds vegetation-driven VOC emissions that affect ozone and secondary organic aerosol formation.
Proper model names
  • European tree-species approach: isoprene and monoterpenes from tree species, biomass density, temperature, PAR and leaf area index.
  • MEGAN v2.04: Model of Emissions of Gases and Aerosols from Nature.
  • MEGAN Plant Functional Types: Broadleaf Tree, Needle leaf Tree, Shrub and Herbaceous Cover.
 Isoprene, monoterpenes, emission factors, LAI, PAR/SSRD, temperature response and PFT maps. Use the European tree-species database where available; MEGAN can also be used depending on setup. MEGAN v2.04 is the practical default for non-European vegetation emissions.
Soil NO, sea salt and dust Adds natural sources that may influence PM, background NOx and coastal aerosol loading.
Proper module names
  • Soil NO: Novak and Pierce parameterisation using soil type and soil temperature.
  • Sea salt: Monahan and O’Muircheartaigh whitecap coverage, Mårtensson et al. fine-mode formulation and Monahan et al. coarse-mode formulation.
  • Natural dust: Marticorena and Bergametti, Mokhtari et al. and Shao-based sandblasting efficiency.
  • Resuspension: traffic-generated fugitive dust and agricultural land-management dust.
 10 m wind, sea-surface temperature, soil texture, soil moisture, snow cover, roughness length, traffic intensity maps and arable-land fractions. Sea salt, soil NO, dust, traffic resuspension and agricultural dust are activated according to the experiment design. Dust and sea salt may be relevant depending on the Mexico sub-domain and July meteorology; module choice should be stated in the run package.
Fire emissions Prevents smoke influence from being confused with industrial or urban pollution during the run window.
Proper fire names

CAMS-GFAS is the standard supported fire product. LOTOS-EUROS also has an interface history for SILAM / IS4FIRES. Fire emissions include trace gases and aerosols and can use plume-bottom height, mean altitude of maximum injection and plume-top height when available.

 Daily fire emissions, species mapping, injection height metadata, fire activity during the selected dates and whether fire is central or auxiliary. Screen CAMS-GFAS fire activity for the August 2024 MACC-domain period. Screen CAMS-GFAS fire activity for July 2024 over the LUWA Mexico domain.
Chemistry and aerosol modules Converts emissions and transported background air into concentrations of ozone, nitrogen compounds, sulfur compounds and particulate matter.
Technical modules
  • CBM-IV oxidant chemistry for ozone, NOx, VOCs and radicals.
  • ISORROPIA2 as the default secondary inorganic aerosol equilibrium module; EQSAM as a faster alternative.
  • Primary aerosol: PM2.5, PM10-2.5, EC, POM, sea salt and dust.
  • VBS approach for optional secondary organic aerosol.
 Tracer selection, aerosol options, chemistry flags, photolysis, heterogeneous chemistry, temperature/radiation dependency and output species. Standard LOTOS-EUROS chemistry package for the MACC run. Same chemistry package can be used once emissions and boundaries are mapped to LOTOS-EUROS tracers.
Deposition and wet removal Describes how gases and particles leave the atmosphere through the surface, vegetation, soil, clouds and precipitation.
Proper module names

DEPAC dry deposition for gases, Zhang particle dry deposition, aerodynamic resistance, stomatal resistance, cuticular resistance, soil resistance, CAMx/Banzhaf-style in-cloud and below-cloud scavenging, and cloud-water/pH-dependent wet removal.

 Land-use classes, surface roughness, vegetation parameters, rain intensity, cloud water, particle size, gas solubility and species-specific settings. Computed from European land cover, meteorology and chemistry fields. Computed from Mexico land cover, meteorology and chemistry fields.

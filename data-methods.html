LUWA integrates satellite observations, atmospheric transport modelling and lightweight emission-estimation methods. The workflow combines TROPOMI-based products, meteorological data, data assimilation and LOTOS-EUROS simulations to support source-level environmental analysis.

Data Assimilation

Integration of observations and model outputs to improve the representation of atmospheric composition, transport and source influence.

Satellite Observations

Use of satellite products, including TROPOMI-based observations, to detect spatial patterns of NO2, SO2, NH3 and related atmospheric signals.

Emission Estimation

Application of lightweight methods and inverse-modelling concepts to estimate emissions from point sources, industrial areas and agricultural systems.

LOTOS-EUROS on AWS

Atmospheric chemistry-transport simulations using LOTOS-EUROS workflows deployed in cloud infrastructure for controlled environmental scenarios.

Plume Diagnostics

Analysis of plume direction, dispersion, source attribution and meteorological drivers using satellite data and wind fields.

Computational Planning

Estimation of simulation domain size, CPU needs and operational feasibility for cloud-based atmospheric modelling experiments.

LOTOS-EUROS Standard Simulation Inputs

This table summarizes the data streams required to configure a standard LOTOS-EUROS chemistry-transport simulation for the two LUWA study windows. It is written for client review: each row states what enters the model, how it is used and what must be confirmed before operational execution.

Europe / MACC domain

Simulation window: 2024-08-12 to 2024-08-22.

Domain assumption: European MACC / LOTOS-EUROS configuration, with MACC/TNO-CAMS emissions as the standard European anthropogenic inventory.

Client use: short-period air-quality simulation with transparent meteorological, boundary, surface and emission inputs.

Mexico domain

Simulation window: 2024-07-01 to 2024-07-27.

Domain assumption: Mexico-focused LOTOS-EUROS domain using global meteorology, global boundary conditions and non-European land-cover inputs.

Client use: feasibility and input-readiness assessment before defining the final production run.

Reading guide: “standard” means that the model uses offline meteorology, prepared emissions, land-surface data, chemical boundary conditions and static surface fields. Exact file names, final resolution and local inventory choice are confirmed during run configuration.

Horizontal and vertical domain LOTOS-EUROS grid configuration Longitude/latitude boundaries, horizontal resolution, vertical-layer structure, model top, output grid. Determines spatial coverage, computational cost, boundary placement and resolution of plume transport. European MACC domain. A standard LOTOS-EUROS European setup can use the default European domain and regular lon/lat grid unless a higher-resolution zoom is selected. Mexico-specific domain. Final bounding box and resolution must match the meteorology, emissions, land cover and boundary-condition availability. confirm domain box Domain size is the main driver of CPU time and operational cost.
Offline meteorology: 3D model-level fields ECMWF-family meteorological files prepared as NetCDF inputs Half-level pressure, temperature, wind components U/V, specific humidity, cloud cover, cloud liquid water and ice water content. Feeds advection, vertical structure, cloud processes, chemistry rates, wet deposition and air-mass calculations. Use ECMWF/CAMS-compatible meteorological forcing clipped or interpolated to the MACC domain for the August 2024 window. Use ECMWF/CAMS-compatible global meteorological forcing clipped or interpolated to Mexico for July 2024. Wind, humidity, clouds and temperature govern plume direction, dilution, wet removal and chemical transformation.
Offline meteorology: surface fields ECMWF-family surface meteorology Orography, land-sea mask, soil type, boundary-layer height, 10 m U/V wind, 2 m temperature and dewpoint, sensible and latent heat flux, friction velocity, surface solar radiation, total cloud cover, large-scale and convective precipitation, snow depth, snowfall, sea-surface temperature, sea-ice cover, volumetric soil water layers 1-4. Supports surface exchange, stability, deposition, dust, sea-salt production, biogenic activity, cloud/rain diagnostics and plume-rise calculations. European surface fields for the MACC domain and August 2024 simulation period. Mexico surface fields for July 2024, including Pacific, Gulf of Mexico and Caribbean coastal cells where relevant. These fields explain when pollution disperses, accumulates, deposits or is removed by precipitation.
Derived meteorology LOTOS-EUROS internal preprocessing Grid-level altitude, layer height, layer thickness, cell volume, air mass, air density, 3D volume fluxes, relative humidity, total rain, cloud partitions, rain intensity, soil moisture index, gravimetric soil water, friction velocity, stability class, vertical diffusion coefficient, aerodynamic resistance. Converts raw meteorology into process-ready variables for transport, deposition, chemistry and emissions. Derived internally from the European meteorological files. Derived internally from Mexico meteorological files. Provides traceability between meteorological forcing and final concentrations or deposition fields.
Chemical boundary and initial conditions CAMS global products, climatologies, EMEP, TM5 or parent LOTOS-EUROS run Horizontal boundaries at west/east/south/north edges and vertical boundary above the model top. Reactive gases and aerosols: O3, NOx, CO, aerosols and chemistry-specific tracers where available. Supplies inflow concentrations and initial 3D fields so that the regional domain remains chemically consistent with larger-scale atmospheric composition. CAMS/MACC-style global boundary conditions are suitable for the European MACC run; climatologies fill missing chemistry-specific tracers when needed. CAMS global boundary conditions are suitable for Mexico because they cover global-scale atmospheric composition. Local nesting can be added if a larger parent run exists. Critical near the domain edges and for pollutants with strong background contribution, such as ozone and aerosols.
Anthropogenic emissions TNO/MACC or TNO/CAMS inventory for Europe; alternative inventories when prepared in LOTOS-EUROS format NOx, SOx, NMVOC, CH4, NH3, CO, PM2.5, PM2.5-10; point and area sources; sector categories. Provides direct source strengths for industrial, urban, transport, shipping, agriculture, combustion and waste sectors. MACC/TNO-CAMS European inventory. Covers Europe and surroundings, including shipping where available. MACC domain Use a prepared non-European inventory, such as EDGAR, HTAP or a national Mexico inventory converted to LOTOS-EUROS format. confirm inventory Main source of scenario interpretation: it determines which sectors and source areas are represented in the simulation.
Emission sectors and speciation Inventory sector tables and chemical mapping tables GNFR/SNAP-style categories: power generation, combustion, industry, fossil-fuel extraction, solvents, road transport, other mobile sources, waste, agriculture. NOx split to NO/NO2, SOx split to SO2/SO4, NMVOC mapped to CBM-IV tracers, PM split into EC, OC, SO4, Na and residual PPM. Translates bulk reported emissions into model tracers compatible with chemistry and aerosol modules. Use MACC/TNO-CAMS sector and speciation defaults for Europe. Apply the same LOTOS-EUROS tracer-mapping logic to the selected Mexico inventory. Allows the client to see whether industrial, transport, agricultural and shipping contributions are separable.
Emission timing and vertical allocation Monthly, weekly and hourly time profiles; vertical emission profiles; optional point-source plume rise Local-time temporal factors, cold-start factors for road transport VOC/CO, vertical layer fractions, stack height, diameter, exit velocity, exit temperature and flow rate where point-source data exist. Distributes annual or monthly emissions into hourly model fluxes and assigns emissions to vertical layers. Use European sector profiles and vertical allocation for MACC/TNO-CAMS sources; plume rise can be activated for point sources with stack parameters. Use Mexico-specific temporal allocation when available; otherwise apply documented default profiles with clear uncertainty flag. Important for interpreting daily peaks, stack plumes and differences between surface and elevated emissions.
Land use and land cover CORINE/Smiatek and optional CORINE 2018 for Europe; Global Land Cover 2000 outside Europe Urban, agriculture, grassland, deciduous forest, coniferous forest, mixed forest, water, wetland, bare rock/sand, tundra, ice, tropical forest, woodland/shrub; DEPAC land-use conversion. Controls surface roughness, dry deposition, biogenic emissions, soil NO emissions, wind-blown dust and agricultural dust. Use CORINE/Smiatek or CORINE 2018-enhanced land cover over Europe. Use Global Land Cover 2000 or equivalent global land-cover mapping for Mexico, translated to DEPAC classes. Land cover explains why deposition, turbulence and natural emissions vary between urban, agricultural, forested, desert and coastal areas.
Topography, soil and land-sea mask ECMWF orography, soil type, land-sea mask and topography-based auxiliary maps Terrain height, soil class, ocean/sea versus inland water, roughness length, preferential dust-source fraction, sea fraction per grid cell. Shapes surface winds, aerodynamic resistance, dust mobilization, sea-salt generation and deposition behavior. European orography and land-sea classification, including Atlantic, Mediterranean and inland-water distinctions. Mexico orography and land-sea classification, including Pacific coast, Gulf of Mexico, Caribbean coast and inland water bodies. Relevant for mountain-valley transport, coastal influence and dust-prone regions.
Biogenic VOC emissions Tree-species map for Europe or MEGAN for non-European regions Isoprene, monoterpenes, plant functional types, emission factors, leaf-area index, photosynthetic radiation, temperature response and canopy activity factors. Computes online vegetation emissions that affect ozone and secondary organic aerosol formation. European tree-species database can be used where available; MEGAN is also possible depending on configuration. MEGAN is the practical default for Mexico because it is designed for regions without the European tree-species database. Important for ozone chemistry under warm, high-radiation conditions.
Soil NO emissions LOTOS-EUROS online soil NO parameterization Soil NO emission factor, soil temperature estimate, land-use class and soil-type dependency. Adds a background natural NO source from soils over grassland, agricultural land and forests. Computed online using European land-use and meteorological fields. Computed online using Mexico land-use and meteorological fields. Useful for agricultural and rural NOx background interpretation.
Sea-salt emissions LOTOS-EUROS online sea-salt generation scheme 10 m wind speed, sea-surface temperature, sea fraction, sodium tracer, fine/coarse sea-salt size bins. Calculates marine aerosol production over salty water bodies and injects sea-salt aerosol into the model. Relevant over the Atlantic, North Sea, Baltic, Mediterranean and other coastal cells in the MACC domain. Relevant over the Pacific, Gulf of Mexico and Caribbean coastal cells. Supports interpretation of PM background in coastal regions.
Dust emissions Land-use map, soil texture map, preferential-source map and meteorological forcing Erodible land fraction, soil particle distribution, soil water, snow cover, roughness length, friction velocity, wind speed and dust size classes. Computes natural wind-blown dust and can represent road resuspension or agricultural land-management dust when input maps are available. Usually secondary for many European urban/industrial questions, but relevant for bare-soil and dry episodes. Potentially important in arid and semi-arid Mexico regions; input maps should be reviewed before delivery. Critical for PM attribution when dust events or dry soils influence concentrations.
Forest-fire emissions CAMS-GFAS fire emissions or compatible fire inventory Daily fire emissions, emitted gases and aerosols, plume bottom, mean injection height and plume top where available. Adds biomass-burning emissions and distributes them vertically according to injection-height information. Use GFAS if fire influence is expected during the August 2024 European period. Use GFAS for Mexico during July 2024; fire influence should be screened because it can dominate regional aerosol and CO signals. Prevents misattribution of fire plumes to industrial or urban sources.
Dry and wet deposition DEPAC gas dry deposition, particle dry deposition, in-cloud and below-cloud scavenging schemes Aerodynamic resistance, stomatal resistance, cuticular resistance, soil resistance, land-use class, cloud water, rain intensity, precipitation, aerosol particle size. Removes gases and particles from the atmosphere through surface exchange and precipitation scavenging. Computed from European meteorology, land cover and chemistry fields. Computed from Mexico meteorology, land cover and chemistry fields. Essential for deposition estimates and for explaining losses during rainy periods.
Chemistry and aerosol modules LOTOS-EUROS chemical mechanism and aerosol thermodynamics CBM-IV oxidant chemistry, NOx, O3, VOC tracers, SO2/SO4, NH3, HNO3, nitrate, ammonium, primary PM, sea salt, dust and optional SOA/VBS. Transforms emitted gases and particles into chemically consistent concentrations and deposition fields. Standard chemistry modules can be applied directly to the MACC run. Same chemistry modules can be applied to Mexico once emissions and boundaries are converted to model tracers. Determines whether the run can answer questions on NO2, O3, PM, nitrogen deposition or plume chemistry.
Satellite observations and assimilation layer TROPOMI products and LUWA post-processing workflows NO2, SO2, NH3 or aerosol-related observational products, depending on the client question and data quality. Used for evaluation, plume diagnostics, emission-estimation workflows or assimilation when configured. TROPOMI screening over the European MACC domain during the August 2024 period. TROPOMI screening over Mexico during the July 2024 period. Provides independent evidence for plume location, source influence and model plausibility.
Operational QA/QC checklist LUWA run-control validation Input coverage by date, missing-file checks, domain overlap, units, vertical levels, tracer availability, inventory year, time-zone profiles, restart/spin-up status and output integrity. Verifies that the simulation is technically coherent before results are shared externally. Check MACC-domain files for 2024-08-12 to 2024-08-22 before launch. Check Mexico files for 2024-07-01 to 2024-07-27 before launch. Reduces delivery risk and makes the technical basis auditable for the client.

Technical basis: LOTOS-EUROS Reference Guide v2.3.000, TNO, December 2023. The table separates standard model inputs from items that depend on the final run setup.

Items to confirm before client delivery: Mexico anthropogenic inventory, Mexico bounding box and resolution, spin-up period, exact meteorological product, boundary-condition product, emission inventory year and whether fire or dust modules should be interpreted as central or auxiliary.

LUWA integrates satellite observations, atmospheric transport modelling and lightweight emission-estimation methods. The workflow combines TROPOMI-based products, meteorological data, data assimilation and LOTOS-EUROS simulations to support source-level environmental analysis.

Data Assimilation

Integration of observations and model outputs to improve the representation of atmospheric composition, transport and source influence.

Satellite Observations

Use of satellite products, including TROPOMI-based observations, to detect spatial patterns of NO2, SO2, NH3 and related atmospheric signals.

Emission Estimation

Application of lightweight methods and inverse-modelling concepts to estimate emissions from point sources, industrial areas and agricultural systems.

LOTOS-EUROS on AWS

Atmospheric chemistry-transport simulations using LOTOS-EUROS workflows deployed in cloud infrastructure for controlled environmental scenarios.

Plume Diagnostics

Analysis of plume direction, dispersion, source attribution and meteorological drivers using satellite data and wind fields.

Computational Planning

Estimation of simulation domain size, CPU needs and operational feasibility for cloud-based atmospheric modelling experiments.

What goes into a LOTOS-EUROS simulation?

A LOTOS-EUROS run is built from several families of data: weather, emissions, land surface, boundary conditions, chemistry and satellite checks. The table below explains these inputs in client-facing language and keeps the technical detail available in expandable notes.

Weather

Wind, temperature, clouds, rain and boundary-layer height define where a plume moves and how fast it disperses.

Emissions

Industrial, transport, agricultural, natural and fire sources define what enters the atmosphere.

Surface

Land cover, terrain, soil and coastline information control deposition, turbulence, dust and sea-salt influence.

Boundaries

Global or larger-domain chemistry provides the background air entering the simulation area.

Europe / MACC domain

Simulation window: 2024-08-12 to 2024-08-22.

Setup: European MACC / LOTOS-EUROS domain with MACC/TNO-CAMS emissions as the standard European inventory.

Use: short-period simulation for air-quality and source-influence analysis with documented inputs.

Mexico domain

Simulation window: 2024-07-01 to 2024-07-27.

Setup: Mexico-focused domain using global meteorology, global boundary conditions and non-European land-cover data.

Use: feasibility and input-readiness assessment before defining a production run.
